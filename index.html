List of Galaxies

The following is a list of notable galaxies. In the Local Group, there exist approximately 51 galaxies, while the Local Supercluster houses around 100,000, and it's estimated that there are 100 billion observable galaxies in the universe. The distinction between galaxies and other interstellar formations like nebulae was elucidated in the 1920s. Initial systematic efforts to catalog galaxies began in the 1960s, yielding listings such as the Catalogue of Galaxies and Clusters of Galaxies, documenting 29,418 entries, and the Morphological Catalogue of Galaxies, which purportedly encompassed all galaxies with photographic magnitude surpassing 15, totaling 30,642. The Lyons Groups of Galaxies in the 1980s identified 485 galaxy groups containing 3,933 member galaxies. Galaxy Zoo, initiated in July 2007, endeavors for a more exhaustive compilation, having classified over a million galaxy images sourced from The Sloan Digital Sky Survey, The Hubble Space Telescope, and the Cosmic Assembly Near-Infrared Deep Extragalactic Legacy Survey. Galaxies lack a universal nomenclature convention, often being labeled based on celestial coordinates in conjunction with the observing project's name (e.g., HUDF, SDSS, 3C, CFHQS, NGC/IC, etc.), prior to definitive confirmation of their galactic status.

Galaxies

Andromeda Galaxy

Constellation: Andromeda
Origin of Name: Andromeda, which is shortened from "Andromeda Galaxy", gets its name from the area of the sky in which it appears, the constellation of Andromeda.

Black Eye Galaxy

Constellation: Coma Berenices
Origin of Name: It has a spectacular dark band of absorbing dust in front of the galaxy's bright nucleus, giving rise to its nicknames of the "Black Eye" or "Evil Eye" galaxy.

Butterfly Galaxies

Constellation: Virgo
Origin of Name: NGC 4567 and NGC 4568 (nicknamed the Butterfly Galaxies) are a set of unbarred spiral galaxies about 60 million light-years away and looks similar to a butterfly.

Condor Galaxy

Constellation: Pavo
Origin of Name: NGC 6872, also known as the Condor Galaxy, is a large barred spiral galaxy and named after a condor, a type of vulture that is one of the largest flying birds.

Comet Galaxy

Constellation: Sculptor
Origin of Name: The Comet Galaxy, a spiral galaxy located 3.2 billion light-years from Earth, named after its unusual appearance, looking like a comet and was found with the Hubble Space Telescope.

Eye of Sauron

Constellation: Canes Venatici
Origin of Name: NGC 4151 is an intermediate spiral Seyfert galaxy with weak inner ring structure and named Due to its resemblance to the Eye of Sauron from The Lord of the Rings.

Antennae Galaxies

Constellation: Corvus
Origin of Name: The Antennae Galaxies (also known as NGC 4038/NGC 4039 or Caldwell 60/Caldwell 61) are a pair of interacting galaxies. Appearance is similar to an insect's antennae.

Needle Galaxy

Constellation: Coma Berenices
Origin of Name: NGC 4565 (also known as the Needle Galaxy or Caldwell 38) is an edge-on spiral galaxy. It lies close to the North Galactic Pole and is named due to its slender appearance.
